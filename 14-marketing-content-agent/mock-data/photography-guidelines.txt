PRODUCT & BRAND PHOTOGRAPHY GUIDELINES
Marketing Team | Style Reference | Version 1.0

=============================================================================
PHOTOGRAPHY PHILOSOPHY
=============================================================================

OUR VISUAL APPROACH:
Modern, human-centered, authentic, and aspirational

CORE PRINCIPLES:
✓ Show real people using technology (not stock photo perfection)
✓ Natural light and environments (avoid overly staged)
✓ Diversity and inclusion in every shoot
✓ Technology as enabler, humans as heroes
✓ Clean, uncluttered compositions
✓ Authentic moments, not forced poses

MOOD & FEELING:
- Confident but approachable
- Professional but not stiff
- Innovative but grounded
- Collaborative and team-focused
- Forward-thinking and optimistic

=============================================================================
SUBJECT MATTER & THEMES
=============================================================================

PRIMARY THEMES:

1. WORKPLACE & COLLABORATION
- Teams working together
- Meetings and brainstorming
- Hybrid work scenarios
- Modern office environments
- Remote work setups

Do's:
✓ Show diverse teams (age, gender, ethnicity)
✓ Natural interactions and genuine moments
✓ Modern, clean workspaces
✓ Technology integrated naturally
✓ Mix of focus work and collaboration

Don'ts:
✗ Forced laughter or exaggerated expressions
✗ Unrealistic "everyone in conference room" setups
✗ Dated office environments
✗ Technology as the focus (people first!)
✗ All-corporate attire (show casual modern workplaces)

2. TECHNOLOGY & DEVICES
- Laptops, tablets, phones in use
- Product UI on screens
- Clean workspace setups
- Home office environments

Do's:
✓ Show our actual product UI on screens
✓ Clean, organized workspaces
✓ Modern devices (recent models)
✓ Natural lighting on screens
✓ Context (person using, not just device)

Don'ts:
✗ Visible competitor products/logos
✗ Cluttered desks
✗ Outdated devices
✗ Glare or illegible screens
✗ Devices without context

3. PEOPLE & PORTRAITS
- Individual contributors
- Team leaders
- Decision makers
- Diverse professionals

Do's:
✓ Represent full diversity (age, ethnicity, gender, ability)
✓ Mix of roles (engineer, manager, executive, etc.)
✓ Authentic expressions
✓ Professional but approachable
✓ Environmental context (office, home, etc.)

Don'ts:
✗ Generic stock photo poses
✗ Arms crossed "power poses"
✗ All formal business attire
✗ Homogeneous groups
✗ Overly styled/perfect

4. OFFICE & ENVIRONMENT
- Modern workspaces
- Meeting rooms
- Casual collaboration spaces
- Home offices

Do's:
✓ Clean, modern aesthetic
✓ Natural light when possible
✓ Plants and human touches
✓ Tech-enabled but not tech-focused
✓ Inviting and comfortable

Don'ts:
✗ Dated office design
✗ Cluttered or messy
✗ Fluorescent lighting
✗ Empty, soulless spaces
✗ Obvious brands/logos (remove or blur)

=============================================================================
TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS
=============================================================================

IMAGE QUALITY:

Resolution:
- Hero images: 3000px wide minimum
- Body images: 2000px wide minimum
- Thumbnails: 800px wide minimum
- Always provide high-res originals

Format:
- JPG for photos (RGB color space)
- PNG if transparency needed
- RAW files archived for key shots

Compression:
- Web: Compressed to 200-500KB (use TinyPNG)
- Print: High-quality, minimal compression
- Balance file size and quality

COMPOSITION:

Aspect Ratios:
- Hero (16:9): 1920x1080px
- Square (1:1): 1200x1200px
- Portrait (4:5): 1080x1350px
- LinkedIn (1.91:1): 1200x627px
- Story (9:16): 1080x1920px

Always shoot wider than final crop to allow flexibility

Rule of Thirds:
✓ Position subjects on thirds intersections
✓ Avoid centering unless intentional
✓ Leave breathing room in composition

Depth of Field:
✓ Shallow DOF for portraits (blur background)
✓ Deeper DOF for environments and groups
✓ Focus on eyes in portraits

=============================================================================
LIGHTING & COLOR
=============================================================================

LIGHTING STYLE:

Natural Light (Preferred):
- Soft window light
- Overcast days (even, diffused)
- Golden hour for warmth
- Avoid harsh midday sun

Artificial Light:
- Soft boxes for even lighting
- No direct flash
- Bounce light for fill
- 3-point lighting for interviews

Color Temperature:
- Warm (4500-5500K) for approachable feel
- Neutral (5500-6500K) for professional
- Avoid cool/blue tones (less inviting)

COLOR GRADING:

Brand Color Palette:
Incorporate subtle brand colors:
- Blue (#0066FF) - Primary brand color
- Teal (#00D4D4) - Secondary accent
- Coral (#FF6B6B) - Tertiary accent

Grading Style:
✓ Slightly warm overall tone
✓ Lifted blacks (not pure black)
✓ Reduced saturation (natural, not oversaturated)
✓ Consistent look across all brand photography

Avoid:
✗ Oversaturation (looks artificial)
✗ Cool/blue tones (uninviting)
✗ Heavy vignetting
✗ Instagram-style filters
✗ HDR look (overly processed)

=============================================================================
DIVERSITY & INCLUSION
=============================================================================

REPRESENTATION REQUIREMENTS:

Every photo set must include:
✓ Multiple ethnicities represented
✓ Gender balance (avoid male-dominated)
✓ Age diversity (20s through 60s)
✓ People with visible disabilities when authentic
✓ Diverse body types
✓ Various roles (engineer, manager, executive, etc.)

AUTHENTIC REPRESENTATION:

Do's:
✓ Cast actual diverse teams/individuals
✓ Show genuine interactions
✓ Represent modern workplace reality
✓ Include accessibility (wheelchair, assistive tech) naturally
✓ Various cultural backgrounds in office environments

Don'ts:
✗ Token diversity (one person of color in all-white group)
✗ Stereotypical roles/representations
✗ Forced diversity that feels inauthentic
✗ "Diversity for diversity's sake" that looks staged

CASTING:

Talent Selection:
- Use diverse casting for all shoots
- Include employees (with permission)
- Professional models when needed
- Real users/customers when possible

Model Releases:
- Always required (written and signed)
- Specify usage (web, print, ads, etc.)
- Archive with images
- Renew for extended use

Approved:
✓ MacBooks, modern laptops (remove visible branding)
✓ Notebooks, pens, coffee/tea
✓ Plants (succulents, greenery)
✓ Minimal desk accessories
✓ Headphones (neutral, no visible logos)

Avoid:
✗ Visible competitor logos
✗ Dated technology (old phones, monitors)
✗ Cluttered desks
✗ Excessive personal items
✗ Branded products (unless our own)

WARDROBE:

Style Guidelines:
✓ Business casual to casual (modern workplace)
✓ Solid colors and simple patterns
✓ Mix of button-ups, sweaters, tees
✓ Avoid: loud patterns, logos, all black/white
✓ Professional but relatable

Color Palette:
- Neutrals: Gray, navy, white, black
- Accents: Our brand colors (blue, teal, coral) subtly
- Earth tones: Olive, tan, rust
- Avoid: Neon, overly bright colors

Diversity in Style:
- Mix formal and casual
- Cultural dress when authentic
- Various professional styles

POST-PRODUCTION & EDITING
=============================================================================

RETOUCHING GUIDELINES:

Light Retouching (Always):
✓ Exposure and color correction
✓ Remove temporary blemishes
✓ Clean up distracting background elements
✓ Remove visible logos (competitors, brands)
✓ Straighten and crop to specs

Do NOT:
✗ Alter body shapes or facial features
✗ Excessive skin smoothing (keep texture)
✗ Remove natural features (wrinkles, etc.)
✗ Change diversity of subjects
✗ Make people look "perfect"

CONSISTENT STYLE:

Apply to All Brand Photos:
✓ Consistent color grading
✓ Similar exposure/contrast
✓ Uniform black and white treatment
✓ Standardized crops for each use case
✓ Watermark or logo (if required)

FINAL DELIVERABLES:

Formats Required:
□ High-res JPG (RGB, for print)
□ Web-optimized JPG (RGB, compressed)
□ Multiple crops (hero, square, portrait)
□ Alt text descriptions (for accessibility)
□ Usage rights documentation

File Naming:
[project]-[subject]-[orientation]-[version].jpg
Example: brand-2024-team-collaboration-hero-v2.jpg

=============================================================================
STOCK PHOTOGRAPHY GUIDELINES
=============================================================================

WHEN TO USE STOCK:
✓ Budget or timeline doesn't allow custom shoot
✓ Specific scenario not available for shoot
✓ Complement custom photography
✓ Quick turnaround needed

APPROVED SOURCES:
- Unsplash (free, high-quality)
- Pexels (free)
- iStock (paid, exclusive)
- Getty Images (paid, premium)

SELECTION CRITERIA:

Must Match Our Style:
✓ Natural, authentic (not overly posed)
✓ Diverse representation
✓ Modern environments
✓ Consistent with brand colors/mood
✓ High resolution

Avoid:
✗ Obviously stock photos (generic business people)
✗ Cheesy smiles and poses
✗ Dated clothing, tech, or environments
✗ Watermarks or low resolution
✗ Overused images (reverse image search)

CUSTOMIZATION:
- Apply our color grading
- Crop to our aspect ratios
- Retouch to match our style
- Add brand elements if needed

Storage Location:
- Primary: brand.company.com/photos
- Backup: Cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox)
- Archive: External drives (key shoots)

Organization:
```

    /headshots
    /team-photos
  /environments
    /office
    /home-office
  /lifestyle
    /collaboration
    /events
  /stock
    /approved
```

Metadata:
- Keywords (searchable)
- Model releases (linked)
- Shoot date and photographer
- Usage rights and restrictions
- Alt text for accessibility

ACCESSIBILITY:

Every Image Must Have:
✓ Descriptive alt text (125 characters)
✓ Caption (if context needed)
✓ High contrast for visibility
✓ Avoid relying on color alone

Example Alt Text:
"Diverse team of four professionals collaborating around laptop in modern office, smiling and engaged in discussion"

PHOTOGRAPHY CHECKLIST
=============================================================================

PLANNING:
□ Define shot list (wide, medium, close-up)
□ Cast diverse talent (age, ethnicity, gender, ability)
□ Scout location (modern, clean, natural light)
□ Gather props (laptops, notebooks, coffee, plants)
□ Plan wardrobe (business casual, brand colors)
□ Obtain model releases
□ Test lighting and equipment

SHOOT DAY:
□ Shoot wider than final crop
□ Capture multiple aspect ratios
□ Get hero, secondary, and detail shots
□ Show product UI on screens clearly
□ Ensure diverse representation
□ Capture natural, authentic moments
□ Shoot in RAW + JPG
□ Check focus and exposure
□ More shots than you think you need

POST-PRODUCTION:
□ Cull to best shots
□ Color grade to brand standards
□ Light retouching (natural look)
□ Remove logos and distractions
□ Crop to multiple formats
□ Compress for web
□ Add metadata and alt text
□ Archive originals
□ Deliver final assets
