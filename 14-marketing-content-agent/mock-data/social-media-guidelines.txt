CRISIS MANAGEMENT & RESPONSE
=============================================================================

NEGATIVE COMMENTS:

Response Protocol:
1. Assess severity (minor complaint vs. major issue)
2. Respond publicly with empathy
3. Offer to move to DM for resolution
4. Follow up privately with solution
5. Follow up publicly once resolved
6. Document in CRM

Example Response:
"We're sorry to hear about your experience, [Name]. This isn't the level
of service we aim for. We'd like to make this right. Please DM us your
account details so we can investigate and resolve this quickly. Thank you
for your patience."

Escalation Criteria:
âš ï¸ Escalate to Manager if:
- Legal threat or lawsuit mentioned
- Executive-level customer complaint
- Viral negative post (>100 shares/retweets)
- Data breach or security concern
- Media inquiry

SOCIAL MEDIA CRISIS:

If a crisis occurs (major outage, security breach, PR issue):
1. PAUSE scheduled posts immediately
2. Notify Marketing Director and PR team
3. Wait for official company statement
4. Post company statement across all channels
5. Monitor mentions and respond with approved talking points
6. Resume regular posting only when crisis is resolved

APPROVAL PROCESS
=============================================================================

APPROVAL REQUIRED FOR:
- Product announcements
- Press releases
- Executive posts
- Legal/financial information
- Partnership announcements
- Anything controversial or sensitive

APPROVAL WORKFLOW:
1. Content Creator: Draft post
2. Marketing Manager: Review and approve copy/creative
3. Product/Legal (if needed): Review technical/legal accuracy
4. Marketing Director: Final approval for major announcements

SELF-APPROVAL:

- Industry news shares (with company commentary)
